Adventures

Brimming with a fascinating culture, boasting spectacular charm, Kandy is the defiant stronghold of Sri Lankan kings. With magnificent Kandyan inspired architecture surrounding this medieval city also famous for the Esala Perahera, (the festival of the tooth), we invite you to explore the best places to visit in Kandy during your stay at Amaya Hills – Our palace in the hills.

Hanthana Hike

Consisting of seven peaks, the Hanthana hike is the perfect experience for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers. The view is absolutely worth the hike with tea plantations, streams, flora and fauna along the route.

The peaks offer spectacular views of Kandy city, the Knuckles mountain range and the hills of the Sabaragamuwa region. The paths are full of uneven sections and the climb is challenging, but the views are truly worth it. The trail is open all year round and is beautiful to visit at any time, with an early morning start guaranteeing mist-capped mountain views.

Peradeniya Gardens

Located in Kandy’s suburbs of Peradeniya, this spectacular garden displays over 4000 different species of plants. The scenic splendor of this garden with the border of the Mahaweli river that flows around its fringes has been a prime attraction for many years.

The iconic Peradeniya Gardens was established somewhere in the 1820’s, but its history dates back to the 14th century. Famous for its splendid collection of orchids, spices, medicinal plants and palm trees, the gardens also nurture over 80 species of birds, 10 of which are endemic, over 80 types of butterflies, 20 kinds of reptiles, 15 kinds of amphibians and 18 types of mammals, making it a true treasure trove to ardent lovers of nature.

Udawattakele Sanctuary

Located behind the Sri Dalada Maligawa in Kandy, this dense forestland spans over 250 acres of rich vegetation and numerous flora. The forest boasts over 400 species of plants, 80 species of birds and a diverse species of butterflies. Ideal for nature lovers, you can spot the rare Three-toed Kingfisher, Layards Parakeet and even the Brown-capped Babbler during your trek through the forestland into its verdant depths.

A historic forest reserve in Kandy, spanning 250 acres is rich with vegetation and numerous flora. Trek through the forestland into its verdant depths and enjoy a peaceful afternoon during your visit to the hills.
